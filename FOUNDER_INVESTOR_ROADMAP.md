## Company Thesis

AgentVault should not be presented first as a broad "agent marketplace." The stronger and more defensible thesis is:

**AgentVault is the permission and audit layer that lets organizations safely delegate narrow, high-risk actions to AI agents without handing over unrestricted keys or credentials.**

The first market should be a narrow, painful, and high-trust category where:

- permission mistakes are expensive
- auditability matters
- existing automation is too permissive or too manual
- buyer willingness to pay is real

Recommended wedge: **crypto treasury and sensitive financial operations**.

## 12-Month Strategic Objective

Within 12 months, the company should aim to prove:

- one repeatable, narrow customer use case
- one productized workflow with strong controls
- multiple design partners using the product in real operations
- at least one paid pilot or paid conversion
- a credible path to enterprise security and compliance readiness

## Milestone Framework

### Milestone 1: Strategic Repositioning

#### Timeline

- Month 1

#### Objective

- move from hackathon narrative to clear B2B control-plane narrative

#### Deliverables

- updated pitch narrative
- updated landing page and README
- narrowed MVP scope
- target ICP and use case selected

#### Success Metrics

- internal team alignment on wedge
- outbound messaging consistency
- at least 10 customer discovery calls completed

#### Risks

- staying too broad
- confusing buyers with marketplace language
- overemphasizing chain novelty over operational value

### Milestone 2: MVP Trust Foundation

#### Timeline

- Months 2-3

#### Objective

- make the product safe enough to pilot

#### Deliverables

- no production demo bypasses
- one real guarded execution flow
- working audit trail and session controls
- operator-visible trust dashboard

#### Success Metrics

- 95% or better successful staging execution rate
- 100% audit coverage on the primary workflow
- zero known silent fail-open behavior in production mode

#### Risks

- execution path remains partially fake
- security debt blocks external users
- payment/settlement story remains unclear

### Milestone 3: Design Partner Beta

#### Timeline

- Months 4-6

#### Objective

- validate real user pain and workflow fit

#### Deliverables

- 3-5 design partners onboarded
- pilot onboarding runbook
- partner reporting cadence
- documented workflow outcomes and feedback

#### Success Metrics

- at least 2 active design partners using the product weekly
- measurable reduction in manual review or execution time
- at least 1 partner willing to expand usage or move toward paid pilot

#### Risks

- pilots stall because product is still too configurable and not opinionated enough
- operators do not trust the UI or workflow controls
- support burden is too high per partner

### Milestone 4: Commercial Pilot Conversion

#### Timeline

- Months 6-9

#### Objective

- convert from free design-partner motion to paid or near-paid pilots

#### Deliverables

- pricing hypothesis
- pilot package
- enterprise-facing documentation
- security and architecture FAQ

#### Success Metrics

- first paid pilot or signed LOI
- at least one strong customer case study
- clear unit of value for pricing

#### Risks

- no clear budget owner
- unclear ROI story
- product is still perceived as experimental infra

### Milestone 5: Category Validation

#### Timeline

- Months 9-12

#### Objective

- prove that the wedge can expand into a category platform

#### Deliverables

- repeatable deployment and onboarding model
- stronger identity/admin controls
- roadmap for adjacent use cases
- fundraising-ready metrics package

#### Success Metrics

- 2 or more paid customers or committed pilots
- strong gross retention signals
- pipeline of adjacent use cases from existing customers

#### Risks

- overexpanding before product maturity
- trying to serve both generic automation and high-trust controls at once
- being outflanked by better-capitalized infra or automation incumbents

## Operating Metrics

### Product Metrics

- successful guarded executions per week
- denied out-of-policy actions per week
- session revocations and pause events
- approval turnaround time
- workflow completion rate
- audit coverage completeness

### Customer Metrics

- number of design partners onboarded
- weekly active partner accounts
- expansion interest within partner accounts
- setup time to first successful guarded action
- support hours per account

### Business Metrics

- paid pilot conversion rate
- average expected contract value for pilots
- founder-led sales cycle duration
- number of qualified opportunities in pipeline
- retention after first 30 and 60 days of pilot usage

## Investor Narrative

### Problem

AI agents are becoming capable of meaningful actions, but organizations still do not have safe infrastructure for giving them narrow, auditable authority.

### Why Now

- MCP and tool-using agents are becoming standard
- organizations want automation but do not want unrestricted delegation
- policy, audit, and trust layers are behind model capability

### Wedge

- treasury and financial operations where permission mistakes are expensive

### Moat

- strong least-privilege execution model
- operator-centric trust UX
- auditable action history
- curated policy templates and certified connectors

### Business Model

- hosted control plane
- workspace or seat pricing
- usage-based execution or audit pricing
- enterprise tier for advanced controls and deployment options

## Fundraising Readiness Checklist

- clear wedge and ICP
- evidence of customer pain from discovery
- live product demo that is real, not simulated
- design partner traction
- pricing hypothesis
- known risks and mitigation plan
- roadmap from wedge to broader category

## Founder Priorities by Quarter

### Quarter 1

- narrow scope
- fix trust-critical product gaps
- align narrative
- start discovery and design-partner recruiting

### Quarter 2

- run pilots
- refine onboarding and trust UX
- prove workflow value
- define pricing

### Quarter 3

- convert pilots
- strengthen enterprise-readiness story
- build repeatable GTM motion

### Quarter 4

- expand within wedge
- explore adjacent use cases
- prepare scale and fundraising package if metrics support it

## Red Flags to Watch

- product demos still rely on internal explanations to excuse prototype behavior
- customers like the idea but do not trust the implementation enough to pilot
- the team keeps adding generalized platform features without proving one workflow
- there is no clear budget owner despite technical enthusiasm
- growth depends on a marketplace flywheel before demand-side trust is solved

## Best-Case 12-Month Outcome

The best realistic outcome is not a general-purpose agent economy platform in year one. It is:

- a trusted control plane for one high-value class of agent actions
- several real customers or paid pilots
- strong proof that safe delegation and audit can become a durable software category
